Volunteer detector listener

Experimental, opt-in. Runs speech-to-text in your browser. Nothing here is required to use the main app.

What this does

If you have a radio scanner tuned to the Union Pacific defect detectors on 160.410 MHz playing into this device, this page can transcribe each detector call in your browser, parse it, and submit the detection to help the live layer. It downloads a small speech model (about thirty megabytes) the first time you start, and that runs entirely on your machine.

Honest caveats: accuracy on noisy scanner audio is hit or miss, the model download is one-time but not tiny, and submissions only count when the app has a detection store configured. The supported and more reliable path is the local watchtower scripts.

Try it

This captures your microphone, so point a scanner speaker at it (or feed audio in). Click start, let it listen, then stop to transcribe and parse the most recent audio.
